## Findings (Non-Blocking)

1. **Gold export is not dedup-aware (EXPORT-01 / DEDUP-01 interaction).** `export_rag_corpus()` filters on chunk-level `substance_passed` but enumerates pre-dedup chunk artifacts, not post-dedup Qdrant points. Cross-document chunks with identical normalized text will appear as separate rows in the gold Parquet even though they collapse to one deduplicated vector. Plausibly intentional (export prioritizes citation completeness; index prioritizes retrieval efficiency) but undocumented as a deliberate scope decision.

2. **Quantitative success criteria unmeasured.** Milestone Success Criteria #1 (<5% garbage chunks) and #2 (<2% junk rows in gold) have no recorded end-to-end measurement against real data — a direct, accepted consequence of scope decision D-2 (forward-only, no retroactive reprocessing of the existing 34-source corpus). `klake quality-audit` (MEAS-01) exists and is capable of producing this measurement, but has not yet been run against a v2.6-processed corpus.

3. **Nyquist validation not reconciled for any of the 5 phases** (see table below) — a process/tooling gap, not a code-quality gap; each phase's own VERIFICATION.md independently confirms comprehensive test coverage by direct pytest execution.
